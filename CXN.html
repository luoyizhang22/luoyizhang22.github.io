â“Background

Chengxinuo is a leading tech enterprise dedicated to improving the living environment of the global BOP (Bottom of the Pyramid) population through green energy solutions. Solar Media is a solar-powered multifunctional educational system that includes a solar panel, two light bulbs, and a learning device. The company mandates adherence to the concept of a "circular economy," focusing on the learning device as the core product to produce a PSSD (Product-Service System Design) concept with market potential. Upon receiving this project, the Solar Media product had already achieved commendable success in the market. However, according to the research report provided by the company, Solar Media currently faces the following business challenges in the market and user experience:

Â· Difficulty in updating knowledge resources, as BOP regions have poor electricity, internet, and transportation conditions, leading to low sustainability of knowledge and poor learning outcomes.

ğŸ“‹Problem Clarification

To better understand the current product usage experience and the mode of the service system, we conducted workshops with 10 children with the support of the company, simulating the BOP population's use of Solar Media and creating corresponding emotional journey maps. Then, based on the Peak-End Rule, we got the following insights:

Â· Multi-user scenarios were not fully considered: This issue is at the peak of the emotional journey. In actual use, given the large number of BOP users, the product usage often involves multiple people, which can lead to conflict situations.
Â· Lack of a feedback mechanism to assess learning outcomes: This issue is at the trough of the journey. "Assessing learning outcomes" is an indispensable part of the learning process, yet the Solar Media product currently does not offer such a service.

Based on these HMW (How Might We) questions, we devised preliminary solutions and created initial system diagrams.




We outlined the following design points as preliminary solutions with the system map. Next, we'll use a series of service design tools to connect these initial ideas into a closed-loop system.

1.How might we empower the product with services to generate concept selling points?
Â· Create a cloud platform to showcase BOP children's learning achievements as "exhibition items" in online exhibitions, drawing public attention.

2.How might we facilitate knowledge resource updates to enhance product sustainability?
Â· Launch volunteer activities in online exhibitions to recruit college student volunteers to create and upload educational content to the cloud platform, rewarding them with volunteer hours.
Â· Utilize local government or schools to help copy new content to personal devices.

3.How might we establish a learning feedback mechanism for multi-user scenarios?
Â· Support content sharing between devices to foster interaction and knowledge exchange among users.
Â· When BOP children update content through government or schools, they need to submit their learning outcomes, which staff will help upload to the cloud platform.

We then developed a service system diagram to link stakeholders and service modules into a system, clarifying the direction of material, information, and financial flows.




This service system forms a closed loop, primarily including three sub-modules:

1.Online Exhibitions
Â· Stakeholders: Exhibition viewers, BOP users, local government or schools.
Â· Process: BOP users bring learning works and Solar Media to local governments or schools for knowledge updates. Staff photograph the learning works and upload them to the cloud platform for viewers to see and support financially.

2.Volunteer Incentives
Â· Stakeholders: College student volunteers, BOP users, local government or schools.
Â· Process: College students register as volunteers, share knowledge content to the cloud platform, and receive volunteer hours and academic credits. Local government or schools download learning content from the cloud platform for BOP users.

3.Educational Support
Â· Stakeholders: Exhibition viewers, BOP users, local government or schools.
Â· Process: Donations from viewers are channeled into an educational support charity fund, part of which subsidizes local government or school staff (mainly low-income teachers), and another part donates Solar Media devices to more BOP populations, promoting a win-win situation for the education system.
